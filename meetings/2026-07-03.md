## Notes

- The meeting is being recorded.  Our code of conduct applies to this meeting.  https://github.com/Ecma-TC54/tg2/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md.
- Intro by Philippe, preliminary matters, proposed agenda.

- Summary
  - The "group" (TC54/TG2) reviewed organizational updates regarding the Open Invention Network.
  - The group agreed to proceed with the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority registration process at a measured pace.
  - The group approved the PURL test v0.2 schema changes to align with ECMA-427 standards, specifically renaming test groups and shifting from roundtrip testing to input validation.
  - Members defined governance criteria for Package-URL type registrations, distinguishing between global types requiring TG2 involvement and community-specific registry processes.
- **Administrative Matters**: Philippe Ombredanne opened the meeting, reminding participants of the code of conduct. The committee reviewed and approved the previous meeting minutes, and Philippe confirmed that the session was being recorded solely for the purpose of compiling these notes.
- **Agenda and Initial Updates**: The committee collected agenda items, with Michael Herzog providing updates and Van Lindberg intending to follow ongoing discussions. Steve Springett requested a discussion on timelines and establishing criteria for PURL types that are submitted to the technical committee, noting that while PURL types are not yet ratified by ECMA, the committee needs to weigh in on them.
- **Open Invention Network (OIN) Adoption of PURL**: Philippe Ombredanne shared an update regarding the Open Invention Network (OIN), an organization involving large patent holders like Google, Microsoft, Red Hat, and IBM. Philippe reported that OIN recently adopted PURL to reference projects protected by their patent pool. Van Lindberg added context from their history with the organization, noting that the move was not problematic.
- **IANA Registration Status**: Michael Herzog and Philippe Ombredanne discussed the status of the Package-URL uniform resource identifier scheme registration, noting the process is complex and involves two levels: provisional and permanent. Michael suggested that securing provisional status serves as a defensible position similar to declaring a trademark, and the committee agreed to proceed with the registration process at a measured pace.
- **OWASP Conference Update**: Steve Springett reported that they were unable to execute their planned agenda at the recent OWASP conference, which included meeting with vendors to discuss service PURLS. Steve intends to reach out to relevant parties in the following week to begin those discussions.
- **Test Overview and Conformance**: Michael Herzog introduced an updated test overview document, noting that it includes a new conformance section. This section aims to provide clarity on test groups by defining what is "required" versus what is "recommended," explicitly referencing clauses 5 and 6 of ECMA-427, and establishing terminology for canonical form and normalization.
- **Normalization and PURL Inputs**: Michael Herzog highlighted that for "base" test cases (conforming to the standard), the input generally needs to be in canonical form, with specific exceptions noted in the standard. Michael emphasized the goal of encouraging users to maintain canonical PURLS to avoid complexity and issues with non-canonical inputs.
- **Aggregator Separator Clarification**: Van Lindberg noted their previous proposal to use the colon as an aggregator separator in 'service' PURLS. Michael Herzog clarified that the current goal is to define the broad rules of the game rather than itemizing every detail, and Van's usage would not necessarily be disallowed by the proposed testing rules.
- **Test Case Management Strategy**: The committee discussed the structure of the test suite, specifically a proposal to move toward having one test case per file to improve unique naming. John Horan expressed concerns regarding the significant management challenge this would create, noting there are currently 521 test cases to convert and maintain.
- **Proposed v0.2 Schema Changes**: Michael Herzog proposed a v0.2 version of the PURL test schema involving three main changes:
  - renaming "base" and "advanced" groups to "required" and "recommended" to align with ECMA-427 terminology;
  - repurposing "expected failure reason" to "expected message" to allow for feedback on normalization; and
  - changing the "roundtrip" test type to "validate" to focus on input validation rather than just input-output parity.
- **Implementation Approach**: Michael Herzog suggested a staged implementation for the v0.2 schema, recommending that the core team perform a major overhaul of existing test cases based on these principles rather than attempting an immediate, perfect solution for every edge case. Philippe Ombredanne expressed support for this approach, emphasizing the importance of providing a roadmap for tool implementers.
- **September Deadline for ECMA-427**: The committee recognized the urgency regarding the September deadline for ECMA-427, with Michael Herzog planning to use an existing project tracker to manage necessary structural changes to the standard and distinguish these from implementation-specific tasks.
- **PURL Type Enumeration and Extensibility**: Van Lindberg inquired about the path for adding new PURL types and whether unregistered types would be allowed. Michael Herzog clarified that the committee does not intend to reject syntactically correct PURL strings simply because a type is not registered, but rather intends to provide feedback messages when types are not found in the registry.
- **Registration Governance Criteria**: The committee discussed the criteria for when a PURL type registration requires direct TG2 approval versus community-led self-service. Michael Herzog proposed that global, general-purpose types like SID require TG2 involvement, whereas specific types with identifiable community registries—like homebrew—should follow a process where the proposers engage with their respective communities for verification.
- **Process Documentation and Versioning**: Steve Springett emphasized the need to write down the registration process to provide clarity to the community, including concepts for "provisional" or "draft" statuses. Michael Herzog agreed to draft documentation regarding governance and suggested that the committee should revisit the topic of versioning for PURL type definitions, noting this would likely be a task to address after the immediate September deadline.
- **Advocacy and Project Status**: Michael Herzog acknowledges a previous question from Van Lindberg, stating that they have been occupied with other tasks but will provide a response by Monday. Van Lindberg reiterated their intent to remain focused on the project's objectives, a sentiment that is acknowledged and appreciated by Michael Herzog and Philippe Ombredanne.
- **v0.2 Document Review and Naming Changes**: Michael Herzog asked the group to review the v0.2 document regarding PURL test changes, which is located in the running notes. Michael explained that the approval of the proposed naming changes, several of which hold semantic meaning, is a gating item required for the next round of project improvements.
- **Impact of v0.2 Changes on Test Schema**: Michael Herzog described how the changes in v0.2 will affect the test schema, specifically concerning the "extra slashes" mentioned in the "shall" statement in clause 5 of ECMA-427. Michael noted that these changes will shift the status of certain test cases from "required" to "recommended," meaning that while tools should handle the problem, the input will no longer be considered canonical. The overall goal for the project is to focus required test cases on canonical input, including strings and object components in the build case.
- The meeting was adjourned.

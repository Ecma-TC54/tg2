## Notes

- The meeting is being recorded.  Our code of conduct applies to this meeting.  https://github.com/Ecma-TC54/tg2/blob/main/CODE\_OF\_CONDUCT.md.
- Intro by Philippe, preliminary matters, proposed agenda.
- Minutes from last meeting: approved.
- Summary
  - Participants agreed to incorporate services into the existing Package-URL schema rather than creating a separate standard. This decision avoids unnecessary industry churn while maintaining a cohesive identifier framework.
  - Discussions included managing the 11-month International Organization for Standardization fast-track process and updating the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority registration change controller.
  - The proposal to implement a new 0.2 schema for conformance-based testing gained traction, replacing complex failure constructs with simplified test messages. Attendees also emphasized the necessity of extensive non-normative documentation.
- **Opening and Administrative Matters**: Philippe Ombredanne opened the TC54-TG2 meeting. They reminded the group that the meeting is subject to the TC54 code of conduct, which emphasizes being nice to each other. The meeting included a review of the previous meeting's minutes and a confirmation that the session was recorded solely to facilitate efficient note-taking, with the recording to be destroyed rather than published.
- **Agenda Setting**: The meeting proceeded with agenda items provided by the participants. Michael Herzog proposed four agenda items (listed in the agenda above), while Steve Springett intended to discuss the status of Package-URL and VERS, the software-id specification, and general timeline comments. Van Lindberg planned to discuss the concept of services as a potential Package-URL type.
- **Services as a Package-URL Type**: Van Lindberg led a discussion on whether to classify services as a Package-URL type or to create a new scheme under the group's standardization efforts. Van argued that since Package-URL already supports schema-specific parsing, creating a new scheme would be unnecessary and would introduce an additional standard for the industry to support. Steve Springett agreed, noting that the scope of TC54 includes software and system transparency and identifiers for services and other supply chain-related things, and that their framing of "package" could be aligned with broader products containing digital elements.
- **Branding and Scope Considerations**: The group discussed the risk of diluting the meaning of "package" if the scope is expanded to include services, hardware, or other entities. The group consensus favored avoiding a new scheme to minimize churn for implementers.
- **Planning for Service-Related Proposal**: Michael Herzog and Philippe Ombredanne requested that Van Lindberg draft an issue and eventually a pull request regarding the service type proposal. Van Lindberg agreed to start with an issue to consolidate the arguments, noting that the resulting proposal would likely resemble their previous ideas but be adapted for "package service".
- **Renaming "sid"**: Michael Herzog proposed renaming "sid" to "software-id" to avoid creating a new, potentially confusing acronym. They noted that "software-id" would be a self-explanatory name. Steve Springett expressed concern that "software-id" might be confused with "swid," which stands for "Software Identification". The group agreed to continue considering the naming to avoid unnecessary acronyms.
- **IANA Registration and ECMA 427**: Michael Herzog provided an update on the IANA registration process. They shared that they had updated the "Change controller" to aboutcode.org rather than nexB, but Eliot Leer has advised that since the registration references ECMA-427, the contact for changing the registration should be an ECMA representative. Michael Herzog committed to asking ECMA for a designated person and organization for this role.
- **ISO Fast-Track Process and Collaboration**: Michael Herzog updated the group on the ISO fast-track process, clarifying that it is an 11-month timeline rather than the shorter timeframe originally anticipated. They advised the SPDX team not to wait for this process to update their treatment of Package-URL. Michael Herzog requested that Steve Springett review the explanatory document for this process, as they wanted experienced eyes on the draft before submission.
- **TC54 Website Concerns**: Michael Herzog briefly mentioned experiencing some confusion regarding the TC54 websites and intended to discuss the matter with Steve Springett at a later time, noting it was not an urgent topic for the current meeting.
- **Test Suite Overhaul Proposal**: Michael Herzog proposed a major overhaul of the test suite, advocating for a shift to purely conformance-based tests while eliminating "advanced" tests that previously caused confusion regarding normalization and recovery. They suggested a new 0.2 schema to distinguish it from the older 0.1 schema and recommended that they work on creating extensive non-normative documentation, using CycloneDX as a reference, to explain concepts and usage.
- **Test Message Structure**: In the proposed test suite changes, Michael Herzog introduced the concept of a "test message" to replace the former "failure reason" construct. They explained that if a test results in failure, the message would serve as the error explanation, while for a success result, the message could optionally provide additional information, such as the use of an unenumerated namespace. This change aims to simplify the structure while keeping input expectations canonical.
- **Test Suite Documentation and Next Steps**: Michael Herzog emphasized the need to develop a table of contents for missing documentation to support the new testing model. They encouraged the group to review the draft document they provided in the shared directory. John Horan inquired about the possibility of adding unique identifiers for each test case, and Michael Herzog noted that while this was deferred for the current phase, it could be considered in the future.
- **Reverse DNS Usage**: The group discussed the use of reverse DNS, with Philippe Ombredanne and Michael Herzog expressing a strong preference for standard DNS over reverse DNS, citing historical complexity and the lack of current practical meaning in the reverse hierarchy. Van Lindberg concurred, noting that they were already moving past the concept of reverse DNS for their work.
- **Parallel Implementation of software-id and service-id**: Michael Herzog suggested that while software-id and service-id could share similar techniques and structures, they should be treated as distinct types to reflect their semantic differences. Van Lindberg agreed, emphasizing that if the semantics differ, they should be defined as different types. The group acknowledged that these parallel types could use similar qualifiers where appropriate while maintaining distinct roles for consumers.
- **Documentation Needs and Closing Remarks**: Michael Herzog highlighted the need for more robust, non-normative documentation to explain various components, such as what defines a "package" or "service". Philippe Ombredanne and Michael Herzog agreed that this documentation is essential for providing clarity alongside the formal specifications.
- The meeting was adjourned.

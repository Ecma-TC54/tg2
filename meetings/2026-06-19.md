## Notes

- The meeting is being recorded.  Our code of conduct applies to this meeting.  https://github.com/Ecma-TC54/tg2/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md.

- Summary
  - Discussions centered on finalizing Package-URL documentation and developing a VERS type schema. Work aims to meet the September delivery requirements for TC54.
  - The group evaluated methods for identifying services and software types, emphasizing the need for formal approval processes for new identifiers. Attendees rejected using experimental prefixes to avoid long-term maintenance burdens.
  - The team prioritized aligning existing test schemas with the 1st edition of the ECMA standard. The group decided to revisit the Software ID proposal in 2 weeks for final review.
- **Opening and Meeting Minutes**: Philippe Ombredanne opened the meeting by reminding attendees of the code of conduct, which is summarized as "be excellent to everyone," and noted that the document is available online. The group reviewed and approved the previous meeting minutes, which were shared in the channel, with the understanding that they can be amended in the future via pull requests if necessary.
- **ECMA Documentation Timeline**: Michael Herzog reported that work on the 2nd edition of the Package-URL documentation is proceeding although the ABNF grammar has stalled somewhat, and they will follow-up with the grammar's contributor. Michael Herzog stated that they expect to have the language ready by mid-August at the latest, noting that no major changes are anticipated.
- **Testing Framework and Schema Development**: Michael Herzog discussed the creation of a VERS type schema, characterizing it as a key step despite not being a formal ECMA requirement. The current plan is to finalize the VERS type definition first, with the potential for creating a schema for the type, though schemas for tests are not slated for inclusion in the standard at this time.
- **TC54 Delivery Schedule**: Steve Springett requested confirmation regarding the timeline for delivering the PURL update and the VERS type to TC54 by September. Michael Herzog confirmed the schedule and agreed to verify the meeting dates and develop milestones for both deliverables.
- **Package Proposal Syntax**: Van Lindberg inquired about coordinating the proposed package and service extensions with the 2.0 release of CycloneDX. Michael Herzog raised a potential issue regarding a previous proposal that used a pound sign as a namespace delimiter, noting that he would review the document. Van Lindberg clarified that a colon was used as the delimiter, and the group discussed that colons should not be encoded in the ABNF grammar.
- **Defining Consensus for Package Types**: Michael Herzog emphasized the challenge of defining consensus for new package types, specifically when there is no established community like the Nix packages group to provide input. The group discussed the need for a formal process where the TG2 task group approves major package types to ensure broad agreement.
- **Service Vulnerability and Identification**: The participants discussed the use of service identifiers for vulnerability lookups. Philippe Ombredanne noted that the community would be "trailblazing" in this space as there are few existing public or private data sources that identify services. The discussion evolved to highlight that identifying services is valuable not just for vulnerability management, but for tracking application dependencies and understanding the "blast radius" or business impact of a service, as Steve Springett described.
- **Approval Process and Community Outreach**: The group discussed the importance of having an official "imprimatur" for new package types to encourage adoption. Michael Herzog and Steve Springett agreed that for major types like SID and service IDs, the TG2 task group should play a role in the approval process. They planned to conduct outreach to industry representatives at companies like Amazon, Google, and Microsoft to gather feedback and ensure these types are useful.
- **Outreach Strategy at OWASP**: Steve Springett volunteered to discuss the service identification proposals with various security solution vendors while attending the upcoming OWASP conference in Vienna. The group determined that referencing the existing discussion item 878 (https://github.com/package-url/purl-spec/discussions/878) would provide the necessary background information for these external conversations.
- **Conference and Logistics**: Steve Springett mentioned that the upcoming OWASP event in Vienna is expected to have the largest attendance of any conference in the region to date. The group briefly touched upon logistics and weather, then refocused on their technical objectives.
- **Experimental Type Prefixes**: Philippe Ombredanne proposed using an "X-" prefix for experimental types to allow for faster iteration without waiting for official standardization. However, Van Lindberg and Michael Herzog expressed concerns that this practice, while similar to MIME types, often leads to "experimental" labels persisting for decades and creating maintenance challenges. Michael Herzog cited RFC 6648 (https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc6648), which recommends against this approach to avoid the leakage of unstandardized parameters into the standard space.
- **Publicity and Community Engagement**: Van Lindberg suggested that writing a blog post and submitting it to platforms like Hacker News could be an effective way to generate community interest and comments. The group agreed that this was a viable approach and determined they could utilize the existing website structure for such announcements once they have a finalized discussion topic.
- **Outreach to External Databases**: Philippe Ombredanne suggested reaching out to the contributors of the Open Cloud Vulnerability & Security Issue Database (https://www.cloudvulndb.org/) to gauge their interest in adopting the proposed service identifiers, which would provide a concrete, practical use case.
- **Defining Private Software**: Van Lindberg argued that "private" software refers to non-distributed, internal code that needs to be tracked within an organization. Philippe Ombredanne countered that "private" is a state rather than an identifier, suggesting that they should use a consistent convention that allows for a continuum between internal and public usage.
- **Internal Tracking in Monorepos**: The participants discussed how to identify software components that are not distributed but live within monorepos or internal environments. Steve Springett argued that distribution is essentially an access control mechanism, and that existing identification methods, such as utilizing subpaths, could already accommodate these internal use cases.
- **Software ID as a General Identifier**: Michael Herzog characterized the Software ID (SID) as a "do many things" type designed to carve out chunks of functionality from the broader, generic category of software. He explained that SID is intended to provide specific identification capabilities that go beyond what the generic type offers, without precluding future refinements or the creation of additional, more specific types.
- **Next Steps and IANA Registration**: Philippe Ombredanne confirmed that the IANA registration request has been submitted. The group decided to revisit the Software ID proposal in two weeks to review it before a final merge, concluding the meeting.
- **Testing Strategy and ECMA Standards Update**: Michael Herzog provided an update on ongoing discussions with John Horan and Philippe Ombredanne regarding testing methodology. They have decided to revert a previously proposed revision plan that included eliminating non-canonical testing for non-canonical conditions. Instead, the current priority is to align the existing test schema with the 1st Edition of ECMA-427 (https://ecma-tc54.github.io/ECMA-427) to ensure it functions correctly before pursuing future improvements. Michael Herzog plans to distribute a new document regarding this strategy later that day and intends to raise the topic for further discussion during the community call.
- The meeting was adjourned.
